Thoughts on Tiny House Movement

For me, tiny house videos and other media around in the “tiny house movement” are irritating because I find there to no real substance or depth. They never provide an answer to “Why (a tiny house)?” that I’m satisfied with. Folks in videos mainly discuss the materials used to the construct the house, information about the appliances or the structure and organization of the house and, some sentimental narrative about the desire to live without worry and to be free. Through the experiences I describe don’t reflect those of all small home dwellers, the disposition of the movement as I have observed is far removed from anything I can find myself giving a shit about. I wonder, why do you get to be free? Free of debt and worry. Free to move across space. What are you doing to ensure others have the same freedom? What are you doing with the money you save and the opportunities of choice with the time you’ve created for yourself to give back to community?

I would prefer, and dare I say love, to see residents and builders facilitate a culture of giving and reciprocity. Many folks involved with or interested in the movement speak about a desire to live with less, save money, have more time to live, help the environment and other pseudo-environmentalist shit that often holds no weight (beyond the benefit to themselves).

I term them pseudo-environmentalist because I cannot see how their choice to “go tiny” saves anyone beyond themselves (for the moment). I believe an advocate for the environment is also an advocate for the people; an advocate for all living beings (mind you, there are plenty of them). What are these folks doing for others who can’t afford the super affordable $10K, or even $2K tiny house? People who have always lived in traditional trailers and trailer parks and always will? What do they do with the rent money they save? Do they give it to the tribes or indigenous peoples whose lands they park on with their tiny house on wheels? What about the Black folks who live in shitty housing on land they weren’t invited to and didn’t provide consent to be on and can’t go anywhere else because of generations of restrictive housing policies?

What are they really doing what I can give a whole-hearted shit about? It’s also important to note that most of these houses aren’t accessible for most people with disabilities. Despite how they may be described as such. An average motorized wheelchair can’t get into the building nor rotate and navigate the space. Ladders and the like to lofted bed areas aren’t navigable for people in wheelchairs and many others. Disabled folks, whether disabilities be intellectual or physical, are often left out of conversations for the greater good. The tiny house movement creates space for more than enough for everyone yet everyone still doesn’t have enough.
